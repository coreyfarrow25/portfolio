## 🔍 What Was Built

### Project 1 — First Look at BeReal
Analyzed BeReal's core product mechanics, user base, and growth trajectory. Mapped the complete user experience from notification to post to social response. Identified the core loop: **daily notification → dual camera capture → 2-minute window → friends feed**.

### Project 2 — Competitive Analysis: BeReal vs. Snapchat
- Side-by-side feature comparison table across 8 core dimensions
- Sentiment analysis of user reviews for two flagship features:
  - BeReal: *Time to BeReal* notification
  - Snapchat: *Snapstreaks*
- PM problem statement: BeReal users disengage when the notification cadence feels arbitrary and the posting experience lacks personal expression
- Opportunity note: Emotional context layer on posts could differentiate BeReal from Snapchat's performative content model

### Project 3 — Feature Development: Mood Prompt
**The feature:** When the BeReal notification fires, users see a one-tap mood selector (5 emoji options) before the camera opens. The mood tag appears beneath the photo in friends' feeds and is aggregated into a personal mood history.

**RICE Prioritization:**
| Feature Idea | Reach | Impact | Confidence | Effort | RICE Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mood Prompt | 9 | 8 | 8 | 3 | **192** |
| Reaction Expansion | 8 | 7 | 7 | 4 | 98 |
| Location Tags | 7 | 6 | 6 | 5 | 50 |

**PRD-Lite highlights:**
- Problem: Posts feel context-free; users want to share how they felt, not just what they saw
- Success metrics: +12% daily completion rate, +8% 30-day retention, NPS lift
- MVP scope: 5 moods, optional, no mood history in v1

**Prototype flow:** Notification → Mood selector screen → Camera → Post preview → Feed with mood badge

### Project 4 — User Testing: Focus Group + Survey
- Designed focus group plan, discussion guide, and 12-question survey targeting 18–28 year olds
- Simulated transcript from a 6-person focus group session
- **Survey results (n = 47):** 74% would use Mood Prompt at least sometimes; top concern was not wanting to feel obligated to share mood

**Key findings from test summary:**
- Mood Prompt resonates most with users who already feel BeReal is "too raw" with no emotional context
- Opt-in framing is essential — mandating mood selection would hurt completion rates
- Emoji design matters more than expected: participants responded differently to abstract vs. literal emoji sets

### Project 5 — Stakeholder Pitch: Mood Prompt
- Built evidence pack with competitive benchmarks, survey data, and retention modeling
- Pitch structured as: Problem → Solution → Evidence → Success Metrics → Ask
- 5-minute pitch script with speaker notes for a simulated PM review meeting

## 💡 Key Takeaways

1. **Clarity beats cleverness in feature scoping** — Mood Prompt won RICE scoring not because it was the boldest idea, but because it had high reach, low effort, and a testable hypothesis
2. **User testing revealed the opt-in framing is the product** — the feature only works if users never feel pressure to use it
3. **Competitive analysis is most useful when you analyze sentiment, not just features** — what users say about a competitor's analogous feature is more predictive than the feature spec itself
4. **PRDs force clarity on success metrics early** — writing the measurement plan before the build spec uncovered two assumptions that needed validation first
